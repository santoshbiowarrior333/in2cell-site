Picking the lead candidate from a screen.

A representative POLEX™ output for 16 DNA-damaging compounds tested in matched cancer and normal cell lines. Each compound is plotted by the DSB burden it induces in tumour cells (efficacy, x-axis) versus normal cells (toxicity, y-axis). Compounds well below the diagonal are selectively damaging the tumour — the lead candidates for further development.

Hover any point for compound details. Drug values are illustrative.

DSB burden — cancer vs normal cells

Lead zone — high cancer killing, low normal toxicity. Prioritize for development. Useful with care — high efficacy but also damaging normal cells. Narrow therapeutic window. Deprioritize — toxic to normal cells without strong tumour selectivity. Inactive — low DSB induction overall. Likely no on-target activity.

Ranked by therapeutic index

#CompoundTI

TI = (cancer DSB burden) / (normal DSB burden). Higher TI means more selective tumour killing. TI ≥ 2.0 marked as lead; TI < 1.0 marked as avoid.

About this view. POLEX™ measures DSB burden directly at base resolution across the whole genome — including centromeres and satellite arrays where short-read methods are blind. The same measurement, taken in matched cancer and normal cell lines, immediately produces a therapeutic-index ranking that conventional cytotoxicity assays (e.g. MTS, CellTiter-Glo) can't deliver because they don't distinguish on-target DNA damage from generic toxicity.
