Aphidicolin: when "not a DNA-damaging agent" is wrong.

Aphidicolin (APH) is the standard tool drug for inducing replication stress in DDR research. Classified as a polymerase-α inhibitor — not a DNA-damaging agent. Most assays show only a modest signal, and most labs use it without considering it genotoxic.

POLEX™ tells a different story. Three matched readouts on the same APH-treated U2OS cells (1 µM, 24 h) show how the conventional toolkit systematically underestimates the actual DNA damage burden — and what POLEX™ catches.

Replication stress = DNA damage.

Compounds classified as "replication stress inducers" (APH, hydroxyurea, gemcitabine, ATR inhibitors at high dose) need to be reassessed for genotoxic liability. The breaks have always been there — conventional assays just couldn't see them at fragile sites.

2

Tox screening blind spot.

Drug developers running γH2AX or Comet assays as their genotoxicity readout will systematically miss replication-stress-induced damage. POLEX™ closes the blind spot by detecting break accumulation at the exact loci where it concentrates — fragile sites, centromeres, satellite arrays.

3

New therapeutic axis.

If replication-stress inducers cause real, mappable DNA damage at fragile sites, then cancers with elevated baseline replication stress (Myc-amplified, cyclin E-amplified, CCNE1) become candidates for fragile-site-targeted therapy — an axis that's been essentially invisible to existing measurement tools.

About this view. Aphidicolin (APH) has been used in DDR research for over 40 years as the standard "replication-stress" tool, with the implicit assumption that fork stalling does not equal DNA breakage. Modern evidence increasingly shows otherwise — stalled replication forks collapse into one-ended DSBs, and those breaks accumulate at common fragile sites, centromeres, and other replication-difficult regions. POLEX™ measures these breaks directly and at base resolution, revealing a damage burden that conventional γH2AX immunofluorescence and Comet assays systematically underestimate because their detection limits sit above the relevant signal at fragile loci.
